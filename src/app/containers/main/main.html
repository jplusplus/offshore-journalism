Offshore Journalism

The right to be forgotten, court orders, pressure from lawyers and outright censorship by police are poking holes in our collective memory. Dozens of articles are disappearing every month and are forever lost.

Our understanding of the past will be dramatically hampered by the chunks of content that disappeared from the web. What if a key element of your next leader's biography was deleted from the archive of your country's newspapers?

Solutions exist. National and private archives can step up. Offshore vehicles can be created to keep a copy of a newspaper's archive. But time is running out. This website is a work in progress, part of an ongoing project to analyze the problem in detail and to explore possible solutions. Here is something to start the ball rolling, let us know your experiences and your opinions.
